Glossary

KeywordsDESCRIPTION
Access PermitWhen people are working on our near the electricity network, they are issued with an Access Permit, which is a paper or electronic form that explains where they may work on or near electricity network assets. These permits must be collected or signed off before the work is considered complete and the network can be returned to its normal operating state.
ApplicantA person who is requesting network access. Applicants trigger the creation of a NAR.
ADMSAdvanced Distribution Management system. A system used to control the electricity distribution network.
AuthorityIn EDNAR, an Authority is used to define a sub-task in a network access request. An Authority will generally correspond to Access Permits issued to field staff to perform the work.
Control RoomThe control room is a secure area where the electricity network is managed for both unplanned work (faults) and planned work, as initiated by EDNAR.
DMSDistribution Management System. Also called the ADMS (Advanced Distribution Management System)
DMS JobA set of operations that is performed “on the fly” in the DMS without an approval life cycle.
DMS ScheduleA set of operations performed in the DMS. Switching Schedules have an approval lifecycle in the DMS, and are created by EDNAR when the NAR is accepted.
DNSPDistribution Network Service Provider. The organisation that manages and operates the electricity distribution network
NARNetwork Access Request – this is the unit of work that EDNAR operates with.
OSSOOperational Spatial Support Officer – a person responsible for updating the GIS and/or DMS. There is a checklist for OSSOs
ParticipantThese are Actors that are involved in the NAR workflow. Participants nominated on the NAR will be sent emails when the NAR state changes, or certain questions are answered.
PermitA document that is issued to someone allowing them to access the network, or work in proximity to the network. Permits generally align with Authorities in the EDNAR application. Also called Access Permits.
